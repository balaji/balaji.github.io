How will the world as we know it will end? i.e., Either the planet is destroyed or it remains intact but the human species gets wiped out.

One [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apocalyptic_and_post-apocalyptic_fiction) article is helpful. To summarize the possibilities here:

* War
* Aliens
* Climate change
* Modern technology
* Resource scarcity
* Natural/Astronomical disaster
* Pandemic

A limitation of this list is obvious. We know only of the possibilities that we can \*think\* of. A few centuries ago, god's judgement day would have been a serious contender whereas modern technology, climate change and resource scarcity would have been absurd. 

War<br> It was a huge threat during the cold war era, now an all-out war is less fancied with more emphasis on short tactical combact. But still, all it takes is one idiot to drop a bomb for the whole world to go berserk. Nuclear enabled terrorist groups would make things much more interesting. But it is hard to wipe out the entire population on war. 

Survival chances: **high**. Occurrence chances: **high**. 


Aliens:<br>I can't see this happening at all. Not because there are no aliens, but because this universe is so huge (human mind cannot fathom such scale: that we are a needle in a haystack in a field of haystacks piled on by another million fields of haystacks which is piled on by another million of haystacks and so on.) and to think that an alien life form that was so advanced enough to travel at light speed to encounter another life form only to destroy it - doesn't make sense. Colonize maybe or a human farm - sure. But blind destruction - probably not.

Survival chances: **moderately high**. Occurrence chances: **very low**.


Climate change:<br> The chances are high, but it is slow and painful, leads to 'last man on earth' scenarios which makes for a great movie, but not for survival. But humans will adopt if its *only* a matter of oscillating high weather of summer and winter, with thunderstorms and UV rays and smog, but this will have a domino effect on other aspects of survival. 

Survival chances: **low**. Occurrence chances: **very high**.

Modern technology:<br> AI, nanotech, genetic cloning, et al. also makes for a good for movie, but can't see that happening. Why? because we don't make such flawless systems, most of our resonably complex systems right from Titanic to Hinderburg to Concorde to Columbia shuttle: we make impressive complex systems but also flawed systems. And it will work to our advantage if and when there is a AI revolution. Elon Musk and Bill Gates may fear this, but unless they knew of secret robotic technologies that we don't know of, it is not going to be in this century. Another reason why an AI revolution is improbable is because of capitalism, cash rich corporates will stifle and kill any innovation if it thinks that the innovation will threaten their existence.

Survival chances: **ok**. Occurrence chances: **low**. 


Resource scarcity: <br>Happening right now in the tropical densely populated countries of Africa, India, Indonesia. Electricity, fossil fuel, farm lands, water - the list is huge. It is only a matter on when. Climate change will accelerate this much faster, the trend of rich people in these countries seem to be to migrate to developed temperate countries of Europe and Americas, but my opinon: any migration to any developed countries won't help. Because a. We are much more connected that we can imagine b. The so called developed countries cannot survive on its own if India, Indonesia (which is, the working class of the modern world) collapse and c. Developed countries are much more irrational and impractical when it comes to survival.

Survival chances: **bleak**. Occurrence chances: **very high**.


Natural disasters: <br>Asteroid impact *will* cause extinction, we all know what happened to dinosaurs. The fact that we know very very little about our neighbourhood in the universe and do not possess enough knowledge to simulate with high accuracy on what astronomical phenomena will occur in the next 1000 to 10000 years, makes this a credible threat.

Survival chances: **bleak**. Occurrence chances: **low**.


Pandemic:<br>Its an evolutionary race between modern medical science vs. the viruses and other germs, put human fallibility into the mix and we have a deadly situation. Zombie apocalypse is a theoretical possibility as well, but mostly it'll be dead bodies, not the walking dead.

Survival chances: **low**. Occurrence chances: **moderately high**.


So from my point of view, climate change triggered resource scarcity will cause extreme distress to human population that may also cause a pandemic. This will be followed by closing the borders of developed(temperate) countries. Temperate countries will barely survive until their economies collapse, as their main scarce resource will be manpower and labour. At a tipping point money will lose its value, majority of the population that were used to first world comforts will fade away and a small group of survivors will go back to being hunter gatherers of the post apocalyptic world. 

(It may also happen that due to our limited knowledge some other threat will cause an apocalypse that we have not thought about. The possibility of humans living in harmony for the next few milleniums is unthinkable at this juncture.)
