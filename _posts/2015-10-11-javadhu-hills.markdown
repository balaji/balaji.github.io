So, its been a long time since I went for a ride (more than 50 days) and I was busy with office work, but finally there was a window of opportunity to sieze and did I sieze or not!


I went to Javadhu hills, I've already been to Jamunamarathur, but this time I wanted to go beyond that and reach Vellore on the other side, and this is how it went.

I was so excited the previous day itself, during such days I don't have to keep an alarm. I woke up by 5, but there were 2 reasons why I left by 7. One, my phone was switched off due to excess youtube watching by Roja and I had to clean my bowel. FYI, I do not have an excellent healthy body and my internals are pretty messed up. I'll cut the story short and say that by 7 both my targets were achieved and I was on my way.

I had a coffee and some sandwiches to start with so I decided to skip 'regular' breakfast. As usual, the porur-ponamallee road is broken beyond redemption due to a lot of hardware industries that needs heavy trucks for transport and very little common sense. So it took a lot of time for me to reach the first checkpoint - Sri Motel Highway. From there onwards there was no stopping, I rode on. My bladder was full due to some of the experiements to clean my bowel and I had to stop once or twice.

Due to the infamous vibrations (I'm never going to buy another Royal Enfield product again until I hear "there are No vibrations!" in the review), the sidebox for the air filter just 'opened' with the lock on (A bit more on this: I always find the sidebox slammed shut so tight to the point that you can't even open it, now I know the reason) because the lock unscrewed itself due to vibrations. I heard noises and I had to slam shut the sidebox after screwing on the lock nut.


![On the way to Jamunamarathur](/images/to-jamunamarathur.jpg)

{: .center}
##### On the way to Jamunamarathur

I stopped on the second toll, bought some cigarettes and continued on - Arcot, Arani, Polur, Jamunamarathur. Then I took a right en route to Amirthi. To road to Jamunamarathur is fun, its a hill road with some twisties and sceneries, I stopped for some pictures, but the real deal was the Amirthi road - it starts with a lot of S bends, one such was a bit steep and I locked my gears and fell at 5 kmph, since it was almost a stationary fall no damage was done. Once the S bends were over, the road climbs down and I had a spectacular view of the hills but a bit scary as well.

![On the way to Amirthi](/images/to-amirthi.jpg)

{: .center}
##### On the way to Amirthi

Here's what google maps tells you - that there is no road between Amirthi and Vellore. It is in a way true - there is a stream that crosses this road and it washed away the road, but the good part is that the road is still crossable because for most of the year because the stream is dry. But since there \*was\* a stream and that there is \*no\* road, the path is soft and hollow. There must've been some rain in the previous days and it has made a ditch in that part. I was unsure whether I can cross the muddy ditch without sinking my shoes in it, but thankfully my shoes were safe and I managed to cross it in one go. This was eventful.

![Amirthi to Vellore on Google maps](/images/amirthi.jpg)

{: .center}
##### Amirthi to Vellore on Google maps

Then I reached Vellore, had biriyani and reached home in one stretch without stopping, the time when I reached home was 4.30ish and it was worth every kilometer. I slept immediately and woke up only by 8 for dinner and now I'm writing this post at night 2.40 AM. Here's to more such rides in the future!
